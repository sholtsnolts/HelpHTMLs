Using dynamic motion simulation with structural analysis

You can use Solid Edge Motion to perform basic motion simulation and dynamic motion analysis on your Solid Edge assembly. You can transfer the dynamic motion load results to your Solid Edge Simulation structural study for finite element analysis.

Motion analysis in Solid Edge

You can use the Solid Edge Motion environment to quickly set up and run motion simulations on mechanical components in your assembly model. Basic motion simulations approximate the effects of motors, springs, contact, and gravity. You can generate animations of your moving assembly, or check for interference as the assembly moves through its full range of simulated motion.

With the advanced capabilities of dynamic motion simulation provided by a Solid Edge Simulation license or Solid Edge Premium license, you can also apply the time-based loads and motion data from the motion simulation directly to your Solid Edge Simulation study for finite element analysis. This allows you to quickly calculate the stresses that result from the dynamic loads without having to worry about specifying load values and locations.

Motion simulation enables you to understand the function of your design inside the Solid Edge environment, before it is assembled with physical hardware. Design validation with advanced motion simulation enables you to build, test, and refine the function of your CAD models. The results produced by this analysis include the same performance information typically gathered through physical examination and testing.

Relationship of dynamic motion simulation to Solid Edge Simulation

Similar to Solid Edge Simulation finite element analysis, you use the Solid Edge Motion analysis interface to:

  1. Open your assembly in the Motion environment using the Tools tab→Environs group→Motion command .

  2. Build the motion model from your Solid Edge assembly. The motion model is a mechanical assembly of separate parts connected by joints that define how the parts move relative to each other. You add joints to connect the parts together, and other motion-specific entities, such as springs and motion generators, to define the physical environment of the moving assembly.

  3. Simulate the motion.

  4. View the motion simulation results. You can display or animate the results in various formats, and check them for interferences. This allows you to review and understand the motion characteristics of the assembly.

  5. (Dynamic motion simulation only) Transfer the resulting forces and moments associated with the motion loads at selected motion frames to your Solid Edge Simulation linear static study. Solving the FEA study shows the detailed results for the effects of motion loads on deformations and stresses for one or more components. You can calculate results for isolated times and time ranges.

    Note:

    For more information about this step, see the section Transfer the calculated motion loads to your FEA study in the help topic, Use motion loads in linear static studies.
