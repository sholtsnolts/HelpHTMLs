What can go wrong—thin regions

Here are some solutions to problems you might encounter when constructing thin regions.

  • Faces selected for offset are not connected to each other.

  • Cap face set too complex. Unable to process cap faces.

  • Thin face geometry too complex to extend to cap faces.

  • Unable to orient cap faces to close offset volume.

  • Selected set of faces do not form closed offset volume. Ensure that cap faces are large enough to cap volume.

  • Unable to offset selected thin faces.

  • Unable to offset selected cap faces.

  • Failed to delete loop on thin face.

  • Thin faces do not intersect cap faces.
